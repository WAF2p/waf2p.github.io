Pillar 6

The Sustainability Pillar

Minimize environmental footprint through efficient resource usage, carbon-aware design, and transparent reporting.

OVERVIEW

Sustainability is an engineering concern

The cloud is not carbon-free. The Sustainability pillar helps teams measure, reduce, and report the environmental impact of their workloads.

Carbon-aware design

Choose regions, instance types, and schedules that reduce carbon intensity without breaking service commitments.

Idle elimination

Stop, right-size, or delete resources that consume energy without producing business value.

Transparent reporting

Measure and share carbon metrics alongside cost and performance so sustainability becomes a tracked KPI.

CAPABILITIES

What the Sustainability pillar covers

From measurement to optimization and ESG-aligned reporting.

Green regions

Prefer low-carbon regions and times of day for batch workloads and non-critical processing.

Compute efficiency

Right-size, autoscale, and use efficient hardware to deliver the same outcome with less energy.

Data efficiency

Tiering, compression, lifecycle policies, and deletion of stale data reduce storage emissions.

ESG reporting

Collect carbon evidence, methodology, and scope boundaries for sustainability disclosures.

MATURITY

Three levels of sustainability maturity

Progress from awareness to carbon-aware optimization at scale.

L1
Baseline

Carbon metrics are collected, idle resources are reviewed, and sustainability is part of architecture discussions.

L2
Standardize

Region selection, scheduling, and right-sizing are governed by policy and checked in CI/CD.

L3
Optimize

Carbon is a tracked KPI, workloads shift to greener regions automatically, and ESG reporting is evidence-based.
